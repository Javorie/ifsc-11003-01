Mission History

A timeline of NASA’s Mars Rover missions

Overview of Rover Missions

This page summarizes the key mission events—including launch dates, landing locations, and major milestones—for NASA’s most advanced Mars rovers: Curiosity and Perseverance.

Curiosity Rover Timeline

Launch

Date: November 26, 2011

Curiosity launched aboard an Atlas V rocket from Cape Canaveral, beginning its journey to Mars.

Landing

Date: August 6, 2012

Using the Sky Crane landing system, Curiosity touched down safely inside Gale Crater.

Early Discoveries

Curiosity found evidence of ancient lake environments and minerals formed in the presence of water—key signs that Mars once could have supported life.

Ongoing Science

The rover continues to climb Mount Sharp, analyzing rock layers to learn how Mars’ climate changed over time.

Perseverance Rover Timeline

Launch

Date: July 30, 2020

Perseverance launched aboard an Atlas V rocket, carrying the first helicopter ever sent to another planet.

Landing

Date: February 18, 2021

Perseverance landed in Jezero Crater, an ancient lakebed ideal for studying past habitability.

Ingenuity First Flight

Date: April 19, 2021

The Ingenuity helicopter achieved the first powered flight on another world.

Sample Collection

Perseverance began drilling rock cores and storing them for a future mission to return to Earth.

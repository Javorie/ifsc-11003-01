Mars Rover FAQ

Common questions about Mars, rovers, and exploration

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rovers has NASA sent to Mars?

NASA has successfully sent five rovers to Mars: Sojourner, Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

What is the main mission of the Curiosity rover?

Curiosity’s mission is to determine whether Mars ever had the right environmental conditions to support microbial life. It examines rock layers, soil composition, and atmospheric chemistry inside Gale Crater.

Why did Perseverance land in Jezero Crater?

Jezero Crater once contained a lake and river delta, making it an ideal location to search for signs of ancient microbial life and collect rock samples.

What is the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity is a small experimental helicopter carried by Perseverance. It achieved the first powered flight on another planet and demonstrated aerial mobility on Mars.

How do rovers send images back to Earth?

Rovers transmit data to orbiters around Mars, which relay the information back to Earth using high-gain antennas and NASA’s Deep Space Network.

Where can I find real rover photos?

You can explore thousands of high-resolution rover images at NASA’s official archive: images.nasa.gov.

Mars Rover FAQ

Common questions about Mars, NASA’s rovers, and robotic exploration

Frequently Asked Questions

How many rovers has NASA sent to Mars?

NASA has successfully sent five rovers to Mars: Sojourner (1997), Spirit and Opportunity (2004), Curiosity (2012), and Perseverance (2021). Each rover has contributed important discoveries about Mars’ geology and history.

What is the main mission of the Curiosity rover?

Curiosity’s mission is to investigate whether Mars ever had the right environmental conditions to support microbial life. It studies rock layers, soil chemistry, radiation, and the planet’s atmosphere inside Gale Crater.

Why did Perseverance land in Jezero Crater?

Jezero Crater once held a lake and a river delta—areas where signs of past microbial life are most likely to be preserved. Perseverance explores these sediments and collects samples for a future return-to-Earth mission.

What is the Ingenuity helicopter?

Ingenuity is a small experimental helicopter carried by Perseverance. It completed the first powered flights ever performed on another planet, proving that aerial exploration is possible in Mars’ thin atmosphere.

How do rovers send images and data back to Earth?

Rovers transmit data to orbiters like Mars Odyssey and the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. These orbiters then relay the information across millions of miles to NASA’s Deep Space Network on Earth.

Where can I find real rover photos?

You can explore thousands of high-resolution rover images in NASA’s public archive: images.nasa.gov. The site includes photos from Curiosity, Perseverance, Spirit, Opportunity, and more.

Mission History

A timeline overview of NASA’s Curiosity and Perseverance rover missions

Overview of Rover Missions

NASA’s Mars rovers carry out long-term scientific investigations to better understand the Red Planet’s geology, climate, and potential for ancient life. This page highlights major mission milestones for Curiosity and Perseverance—two of NASA’s most advanced robotic explorers.

Curiosity Rover Timeline

Launch

Date: November 26, 2011

Curiosity launched aboard an Atlas V rocket from Cape Canaveral, beginning its eight-month journey to Mars.

Landing

Date: August 6, 2012

The rover successfully touched down in Gale Crater using the revolutionary Sky Crane landing system—one of NASA’s most complex landing procedures ever attempted.

Early Discoveries

Curiosity found evidence of ancient lakes, minerals formed in water, and organic molecules—strong indicators that Mars once had conditions suitable for microbial life.

Ongoing Science

Curiosity continues to ascend Mount Sharp, examining layered rock formations that reveal how Mars’ climate has changed over billions of years.

Perseverance Rover Timeline

Launch

Date: July 30, 2020

Perseverance launched aboard an Atlas V rocket on a mission to search for signs of ancient life and collect samples for future return to Earth.

Landing

Date: February 18, 2021

The rover landed in Jezero Crater, a region that once contained a river delta—ideal for preserving evidence of past microbial life.

Ingenuity First Flight

Date: April 19, 2021

The Ingenuity helicopter achieved the first powered flight on another planet, demonstrating the potential of aerial exploration on Mars.

Sample Collection

Perseverance began drilling and storing rock samples, which may eventually be returned to Earth for detailed laboratory analysis.

exploring the natural state’s wild side

arkansas truly lives up to its nickname, the natural state, offering some of the most diverse and rugged outdoor experiences in the midwest and south. From the ancient, rolling ozark mountains to the challenging ouachita national forest, the opportunities for adventure are vast. This guide covers some of the most essential destinations and activities for the serious adventurer.

the buffalo national river region: a paddler's and hiker's paradise

the buffalo national river, established in 1972 as america’s first national river, is a spectacular destination. its towering limestone bluffs, pristine waters, and accessible gravel bars make it a premier spot for multi-day paddling trips and scenic hiking.

key hiking trails in the upper region

the area around jasper and ponca offers incredible access to trails leading to panoramic views. the lost valley trail is a popular beginner-friendly choice, while endurance athletes may prefer longer routes with rapid elevation changes.

gear checklist tips for multi-day excursions

when planning a multi-day trip, preparation is everything. keep pack weight down and prioritize safety equipment.

  • water filtration system: crucial for safety and reducing pack weight
  • emergency shelter: lightweight tarp or bivy sack
  • headlamp: always carry extra batteries

rock climbing and bouldering in the ozarks

the ozarks boast sandstone and limestone formations perfect for climbing. sam’s throne and horseshoe canyon ranch are well-known among climbers.

essential activities for arkansas adventurers

  • trail running (foyil trail)
  • paddling (canoeing, kayaking, rafting)
  • rock climbing (sport and trad)
  • backpacking and camping
  • mountain biking

unscheduled events can occur; experienced guides always stress the need to "be prepared for everything, expect nothing."
